Richard Gere A Tired Report

These poems are written spontaneously to release emotion. I urge the reader to do the same.

BIKING WITHOUT THE SUN
The high-beam-headlights of passing vehicles reflect off my glasses.
Dark, perfect blackness contrasts the refracted rays of glitter.
The ocean and sky would blend into one unanimous shade of blue charcoal. I'm on the edge of the earth, gliding over the invisible tar.
No street lights to guide me, no sunlight to help me, I was all alone as my wheels flew me over this empty stretch of land.
Potholes sprinkle the uninhabited road.
I'm so grateful.
GOING HOME
I love night-time drives,
When the dark looms and hums.
The air loosens it's grind.
Flooding down my lungs.

I see the ocean to my right,
As it stretches from sea to sea.
The ocean at this time of night
Slows and brings peace.

My eyes take it in, the whole panorama.
I feel the air thinning.
Deep, full breaths, I dump and draw.
The blanket of cold layers intimately.

My whole body melts into the cushions,
And finally my eyes can see,
My narrow lens starts to pull in
The darkness out around me.

Bigger, bigger it opens,
Like a flower in a garden.
I feel like I was the king.
Before the current's closing.

The ocean bends and slows
The time that I just spent,
Night skies reveal and show,
The places we used to frequent.

Now I know I know
The times of my very end
Now I know I know
I'm not scared to change the lens.
THE DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION
I miss the single-digit days
When it was simple,
On the bus, I stayed
Watching lights outside my window twinkle.

Small, single-digit days of old,
I would be a passenger in my own life.
By my bus, I was controlled,
But I loved to see those lights.

Drawn closer to the light,
We crawled out of the bus, moving,
Praised to be of height.
They called it growing.

Cheers came from outside the pane.
I, as most did, left the moving bus
Knowing that the fall out would stain
My body with scratches and cuts.

Start walking. I can't just sit here.
Rising to my feet, I stumbled.
My legs would give, I feared.
But it was too late to stop and fumble.

I had to keep moving forward.
I wanted this feat,
But it's so much easier to move toward.
A goal when your in a bus seat.
FREQUENT FLYERS
The confusion of living, breathing minds
is taken off the backs of many who found
to frequent the skies above, choose to resign
From friends who want to see them tread the ground.

Do I fly more than I walk minds with minds
with you, who makes me feel so tall and far?
In that I grab at your feet from the skies
please trust, I don't want to be left alone here.

To be on my flight alone pulls the clouds between us, but I'm scared to fly higher,
The clouds below make me afraid of becoming what some may call a frequent flyer.
ROSE-TINTED
The smell of the afternoon sun
And orange lights of excitement
Would blur my morning fun
For the afternoon assignments.

But after the work was done
And we packed up for the next day,
Our world would have begun,
And our game would be underway.

Hours and hours and days and weeks,
Consumed by love to talk and fight,
We chose to skip hours of sleep,
In favor of familiar, strobing lights.

Now we're old, grumpy, and grown
Working to support our days of old,
Afraid to look back to happier times,
the times of flowers and rusting gold.
GRANDMA'S EYES
Oceans of white doomed to dry
Against the brazen sky,
The storm of sight needs the night
To love the bluest dyes.

Twin moons come, struggle, and tug
To close the beady eye.
It burns against fiery sun,
Hoping not to bleed and die.

A life that's long or short as mine

Don't fall for the visage of phony heaven
Really, what a shame

Before we douse the flame.

INSIDE ROOM 303A

Every now and then, I visited her.
I shouldn't have came back.

She died a few months ago.
It didn't feel like a special day.

I would be by her old rehab center

TO DESIRE BEAUTY
Imagine a world where eyes could taste
The things we see and try to eat
Your favorite pie from grandmother's plate
A picture you saved to watch and feast.

Now imagine a fruit with glowing hues
Of red and green and yellow and blue.
Sweet and succulent it's juice must be
But it's sheen is hard and glassy.

I want to bite the fluorescent apple,
And taste it's exuberant pigments,
But I know my teeth will shake and raddle
Until my mouth breaks and splints.

So I will watch the forbidden fruit,
with tasting eyes of starving envy.
Hoping the apple will claim me the food
Grow a mouth of its own and eat me.
PHILANTHROPISTS: COMPLICIT AND PERFORMATIVE
We watch the people starved for food
in need of grocery money
scream in rejoice and gratitude
for what we gave them, a vat of honey.

My nails are buried in a leathery neck,
hands coupled around crocodile skin,
wrists are borne down on by the peck
of tears that roll down his vile grin.

"Stop smiling. It's not funny."
I say to the man who giggles,
"Look, look, they're stuck in honey!"
He says with eyes the shape of sickles.

Left hand on the right,
Right hand on the left,
Why am I laughing at this sight?
I don't know, hold my breath.
PROMETHEUS WILL DIE
Excited we are for the coming celebration
As we rest on the labors of our year,
Amidst our communal retrospection,
Comes the scared little winter deer.

Cracked, the antlers have been, for so long,
Bloody and cut thin like sticks,
The hammers and axes sing the song
Of Prometheus as they cut bit by bit.

Crack and crick and notch and nip,
The bones we harvest and scan,
Happy, we leave, with all the chips
Of boney gold in our hands.

The crippled deer, sad and broken, hides away
To heal his missing horns.
The antlers grow with cracks that ache
Afraid of the next winter storm.

It's not like we don't know where the cracks lead.
They beg us to ask the question:
What will we do when it's time to leave our home of burning heaven?

BEING THE POT THAT BOILS
The dog I let to eat its native prey,
I can't restrict the native wants of life
gave birth, to eat and sleep and drink and play
all things for which we thirst in fear of strife.

As well, I can't remove the want from me,
Less human than human, for it's you I can do without,
so I shall be the pot that boils of free
the taste to palate and prize in your mouth.

How sad it is to be the pot that boils,
up until my drink becomes mother's soil.
